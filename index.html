Hi, I'm Anurag!

I am a PhD student in the CSIR-Institute of Genomics and Integrative Biology in New Delhi, India.

I work on the identification of human proteoforms under the guidance of Dr Debasis Dash. I'm broadly interested in understanding the role of human tissue specific proteoforms in the light of mass spectrometry.

Prior to this, I completed my Masters degree in Bioinformatics from Pondicherry University in Puducherry, India.

I grew up in Arrah, Bihar, famous for its language "Bhojpuri" in India. I did Bachelor in Science from Dyal Singh College, under Delhi University, New Delhi.

Apart from all this, I am an avid traveller and photographer (check out my Instagram profiles for a showcase). When I am not working, you will find me doing one of these things: planning trips, reading books, watching movies or sleeping.

Research Statement

My research revolves around making sense of proteoforms with mass spectrometry datasets with respect to the following directions:

  • Identifying proteoforms in different human tissues. We have developed novel computational framework to find out how a proteoforms which generally missed out in normal proteomic identification can be identified with changing the search database.

  • Developing scalable computational and statistical tools to peptides identified through datasets in the field of Mass spectrometry.

Apart from this, I am also interested in developing small tools for easy handling the big data.
